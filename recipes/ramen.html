Ramen

Ingredients

  • Pork Belly 500g
  • Regular Soya Sauce (Koikuchi Shoyu) 375ml
  • Cooking Wine 250ml
  • Honey 50g
  • Garlic (Grated)
  • Ginger (Grated)
  • Fresh Eggs (4 pcs)
  • Katsuoboshi (Fish Flakes) 20-30g
  • Water (for marinade and broth) 3.2L+
  • Kombu 1pc
  • Dried Shitake Mushrooms 3pc
  • Mixed Dried Fish (Katsuo, Saba, Aji) 100g
  • Mirin 60ml
  • Light Soya Sauce (Usukuchi Shoyu) 100ml
  • Thai Fish Sauce 25ml
  • Apple Cider Vinegar 10ml
  • Okinawa Sea Salt 50g
  • Fat from Fresh Pork Back 200g
  • Leek (Negi)
  • Whole Chicken 1pc
  • Yellow Onion 1/2 pc
  • Carrot 1pc
  • Celery 1pc
  • Top Flour 200g
  • Salt 2g
  • Kansui 2g
  • Corn Starch (Small Portion)
  • Spinach
  • Seaweed 1pc

Steps

Step 1: Chashu

  • Ingredients:
    • Pork Belly 500g
    • Chashu Sauce:
      • Regular Soya Sauce (Koikuchi Shoyu) 250ml
      • Cooking Wine 250ml
      • Honey 30ml
      • Garlic (Grated)
      • Ginger (Grated)
  1. Slice pork belly in half
  2. Set stove to high heat and place pork belly into the fry pan
  3. Turn the meat every few minutes to evenly grill all sides
  4. When all sides are evenly charred, set aside on a plate to cool
  5. Combine all ingredients for Chashu Sauce in a pot and boil at medium heat
  6. When sauce starts boiling, add pork belly into pot
  7. Cover and simmer for 30 min
  8. Turn pork belly and simmer another 30 min to marinate the other side
  9. Remove from pot to cool, and store in the refrigerator

Step 2: Ajitama

  • Ingredients:
    • Fresh Eggs (4 pcs)
    • Katsuoboshi (Fish Flakes) 10g
    • Water 200ml (Ajitama Marinade)
    • Chashu Marinade 200ml (from Step 1)
  1. To start preparing Ajitama Marinade, boil water with high heat
  2. When water is boiling, add katsuoboshi and switch off the stove
  3. Soak katsuoboshi for about 30s
  4. Filter katsuoboshi with a strainer and Katsuo Dashi is done
  5. Combine Katsuo Dashi with Chashu Sauce to complete Ajitama Marinade
  6. Heat water to boiling point
  7. Add fresh eggs and boil for 6 min without switching off the fire
  8. Remove eggs and immediately place into an ice bath, this will create a beautiful yolk color
  9. When eggs have cooled down, peel eggs and soak in Ajitama Marinade
  10. Store in refrigerator to marinate for at least 1-2 days

Step 3A: Ramen Tare (Sauce)

  • Ingredients:
    • Dashi 250ml:
      • Kombu 1pc
      • Dried Shitake Mushrooms 3pc
      • Mixed Dried Fish (Katsuo, Saba, Aji) 100g
      • Water 1L
    • Mirin 60ml
    • Regular Soya Sauce (Shoyu) 125ml
    • Light Soya Sauce (Usukuchi Shoyu) 100ml
    • Thai Fish Sauce 25ml
    • Apple Cider Vinegar 10ml
    • Okinawa Sea Salt 50g (Regular Salt can be used too)
    • Honey 20g
  1. To make Dashi:
    1. Combine all dashi ingredients and soak overnight in refrigerator
    2. Set aside 250ml to make Ramen Tare
  2. Heat dashi in stove with kombu
  3. When dashi is beginning to boil, remove kombu
  4. Skim any scum from the dashi and continue to simmer for 20 min
  5. Filter with a strainer and dashi is done
  6. Combine the rest of ingredients with dashi and boil (stir mixture well)
  7. When mixture boils, turn off the stove and let Ramen Tare cool down before setting aside

Step 3B: Negi Oil

  • Ingredients:
    • Water
    • Fat from Fresh Pork Back 200g
    • Leek (Negi)
  1. Pour water into fry pan and heat at small-medium fire
  2. Slowly add pork fat into pan
  3. Break down pork fat into smaller pieces when it starts to cook
  4. Add more water and continue to fry
  5. When water evaporates, pork oil starts to form
  6. Continue frying until the pork lard is golden brown in color
  7. Stir occasionally so that more pork oil will come out
  8. When pork lard is soaked in pork oil, turn off stove and strain pork oil into a bowl
  9. Dice leek (Negi)
  10. Heat pork oil in a saucepan at low-medium fire
  11. Add diced negi and stir well from time to time
  12. Cook until negi turns golden brown (Do not burn it)
  13. Place saucepan into an ice bath and stir to cool

Step 4: Chicken Soup (Broth)

  • Ingredients:
    • Whole Chicken 1pc
    • Yellow Onion 1/2 pc
    • Ginger (Small Portion)
    • Garlic (Small Portion)
    • Carrot 1pc
    • Celery 1pc
    • Katsuoboshi (Fish Flakes) 10-20g
  1. Chop chicken into smaller pieces for boiling
  2. In a large pot, add water (2 to 2.5L) until chicken is fully covered and heat
  3. When water starts boiling, remove scum and simmer for 2 hours
  4. Add remaining ingredients into soup and continue to simmer for 1 hour
  5. Stop fire and remove chicken from the soup
  6. Bring remaining soup to a boil again, add katsuoboshi and soak for 5 min
  7. Strain chicken soup into a bowl placed in an ice bath to cool, then set aside

Step 5: Noodle

  • Ingredients:
    • Top Flour 200g
    • Water 80ml
    • Salt 2g
    • Kansui 2g
    • Corn Starch (Small Portion)
  1. Mix water, salt, and kansui
  2. Slowly mix the top flour in 2-3 batches
  3. Wrap the mixture to prevent drying and let it stand for 30 min
  4. Knead the mixture into dough
  5. Flatten and fold repeatedly using a ziplock bag
  6. Wrap with cling wrap and let it stand for 40 min
  7. Use a roller to flatten and cut into long rectangular pieces to fit the noodle machine
  8. Flatten dough progressively with the machine
  9. Add corn starch and switch to noodle dial to cut noodles

Step 6: Ramen Topping

  • Ingredients:
    • Leek (Negi)
    • Spinach
    • Chashu
    • Seaweed 1pc
    • Ajitama 1pc
  1. Dice negi and soak in water for 5 min
  2. Blanch spinach in boiling water for 1 min
  3. Cool spinach in an ice bath
  4. Slice chashu (2-3 pieces per serving)

Step 7: Shoyu Ramen

  1. Reheat chicken soup on stove
  2. Add 25ml Ramen Tare (Sauce) to serving bowl
  3. Add diced negi to the bowl
  4. Sprinkle 1 tbsp negi oil and add some fried negi bits
  5. Cook 130g noodles in boiling water for 1 min
  6. Add 300ml chicken soup to the bowl
  7. Drain noodles well and place them into the bowl
  8. Arrange noodles neatly using chopsticks
  9. Add chashu slices, ajitama, spinach, and seaweed as toppings
  10. Shoyu Ramen is ready to serve
